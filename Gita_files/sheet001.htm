मामकाः     पाण्डवाश्चैव            किमकुर्वत          सञ्जय ||1.1||
māmakāḥ pāṇḍav-āśhchaiva    kim-akurvata       sañjaya
আমার    এবং পান্ডূপুত্রগ্ণ  কি করল          সন্জয়

Adharma destroys Kulam
অজ্ঞানরুপি ধ্রিতরাষ্ট্র
সংযম্রুপি সন্জয়		 Holy since tapasya by Brahma, Devas, Rishis + 8 dharmic virtues planted by Raja Kuru - satya, tapa,  shaucha, yoga, brahmacharya, daya, kshama, daan; One dying here during tapas or battle will attain moksha by blessings of Indra / Vishnu.
King Dhritarashtra differentiates among his family and equates his adharmic sons with the Pandavas, both desiring war. But Pandavas gathered for Dharma, Kauravas for kingdom.
Six Ripus (evils) - Kaam, Krodh, Madh, Lobh, Moha, Matsarya.
2 सञ्जय दृष्ट्वा      तु    पाण्डवानीकं       व्यूढं            दुर्योधनस्तदा ।

आचार्यमुपसङ्गम्य             राजा   वचनमब्रवीत् ।। 1.2।।
āchāryam-upasaṅgamya   rājā     vachanam-abravīt
আচার্যের কাছে             রাজা কথা বললেন    
To Guru-approached    (as)king     words-spoke		 Dharma strengthens / Adharma weakens Pandavas had 7 Akshauhini sena vs larger Kaurav 11 Akshauhini sena. But appeared more formidable and in unison since they were acting with Dharma.

The Guru had a soft corner for the Pandavas. But, Duryodhana, as king, needed to know the Guru would fight the powerful Pandav army without any laxity.

व्यूढां         द्रुपदपुत्रेण           तव   शिष्येण       धीमता ।। 1.3।।
vyūḍhāṁ   drupada-putreṇa tava śhiṣhyeṇa     dhīmatā
ব্যুহরচিত  দ্রুপদপুত্রেণ     তব  শিষ্যেণ      বুদ্ধিমান
arranged Drupada-son(by)  your   student(by)   intelligent		 Dharma strengthens / Adharma weakens Adharmic Duryodhana tries to sully Guru Drona's mind towards Pandavas and Dhristadyumna. He indirectly demeans the Guru by suggesting he was a fool to teach shastra-vidya to Dhristadyumna, the son of his old rival Drupad, and born to be the cause of his death. Duryodhana does not appreciate his Guru's self-confidence and charity in teaching even his rival's son.

युयुधानो     विराटश्च       द्रुपदश्च           महारथ: || 1.4||
yuyudhāno virāṭaśhcha drupadaśhcha mahā-rathaḥ

Yuyudhan    Virat(and)   Drupad(and)     masters(of war)		 Dharma reduces ego/ Adharma incites ego Adharmic Duryodhana tries to incite Guru Drona's ego by praising the opponents' strengths. He cites Yuyudhan (Satyaki) to show how a disciple should fight alongside his Guru (unlike Drupad's son); Virat, to remind how Drona lost against his army; and lastly Drupad to subtly re-open old wounds. [Only Satyaki survived the war; Virat and Drupad died in battle]
5 सञ्जय: दुर्योधन धृष्टकेतुश्चेकितान:                       काशिराजश्च        वीर्यवान् |
dhṛiṣhṭaketuśh-chekitānaḥ     kāśhirājaśh-cha    vīryavān
Dhrishtaketu(and) Chekitan    KashiRaj(and)        heroic

पुरुजित्कुन्तिभोजश्च            शैब्यश्च              नरपुङ्गव: || 1.5||
purujit-kunti-bhojaśh-cha    śhaibyaśh-cha    nara-puṅgavaḥ
Purujit KuntiBhoj-and           Shaibya-and        men-best of		 Dharma enlightens/ Adharma blinds Duryodhana lists warriors he thinks are mad or biased. He is blind to the fact they chose the side of Dharma. Dhrishtaketu's father Sisupala was slain by Krishna; Chekitan did not join the Yadav army on Kaurav's side; Kashiraj, Purujit & Kuntibhoj, and Shaibya were all family and known to be wise, but all chose Pandava's side. 
6 सञ्जय: दुर्योधन युधामन्युश्च              विक्रान्त  उत्तमौजाश्च          वीर्यवान्
yudhāmanyuśh-cha vikrānta  uttamaujāśh-cha  vīryavān
Yudhamanyu-and    bold,         Uttamauja-and     heroic       

सौभद्रो               द्रौपदेयाश्च              सर्व    एव   महारथा: || 1.6||
saubhadro           draupadeyāśhcha   sarva eva    mahā-rathāḥ
Subhadra's-son   Draupadi's sons-&  all      truly  masters(of war)		 Dharma shines/
Adharma cheats; Dharmic people can under guidance of God/Guru bend rules without violating dharma.		 Duryodhana lists younger next generation master warriors Yet he desires victory against them. His confidence stems from his having cheated to victory in the past (rigged game of dice made Pandavas lose their kingdom to him). He would cheat to win again. Later, his last general Ashwathama kills these young warriors in their sleep thinking them to be the Pandavas. However, lest the Guru gets irritated and asks for a truce, he goes on to list their own warriors.
7 सञ्जय: दुर्योधन अस्माकं    तु     विशिष्टा  ये      तान्निबोध           द्विजोत्तम |
asmākaṁ  tu     viśhiṣhṭā ye      tān-nibodha         dwijottama
Ours          but    special   ones   them-focus(on)   Brahmin

नायका मम      सैन्यस्य     संज्ञार्थं             तान्ब्रवीमि    ते || 1.7||
nāyakā mama   sainyasya  sanjñārthaṁ     tān-bravīmi   te
leaders our        army(of    (to)notify(you)   them-tell-i      (to)you		 Dharma rewards truth and ability/ Adharma rewards sycophancy & loyalty  Duryodhana asks Brahmin Guru Drona to focus his attention on the special warriors in their own army. He lists the leaders of his army. These are warriors he esteems and knows as loyal.  While listing them he gives a subtle hint that there are warriors here, who could be placed above Guru Drona in rank if Drona did not perform as expected.
8 सञ्जय: दुर्योधन भवान्भीष्मश्च                 कर्णश्च          कृपश्च         समितिञ्जय: |

अश्वत्थामा       विकर्णश्च        सौमदत्तिस्तथैव            च || 1.8||
aśhvatthāmā  vikarṇaśhcha  saumadattis-tathaiva   cha
Ashwattama   Vikarna(and)   Saumadat-son-even     and		 Dharma can win against all odds/ Adharma loses with odds in favor Duryodhana mentions Drona first then Bheeshma to boost the Guru's ego and win his loyalty. He mentions his bosom friend Karna next but calls Kripacharya battle-victorous so as not to offend. Both Kripacharya and Ashwatthama (Drona's son) are immortals. The he lists Vikarna and Saumadattis son, Bhurishrava, who are both considered dharmic. [However, both die in battle]

36 सञ्जय: निहत्य धार्तराष्ट्रान्न: का प्रीति: स्याज्जनार्दन |
पापमेवाश्रयेदस्मान्हत्वैतानाततायिन: || 36 ||
तस्मान्नार्हा वयं हन्तुं धार्तराष्ट्रान्स्वबान्धवान् |
स्वजनं हि कथं हत्वा सुखिन: स्याम माधव || 37||
nihatya dhārtarāṣhṭrān naḥ kā prītiḥ syāj janārdana
pāpam evāśhrayed asmān hatvaitān ātatāyinaḥ
tasmān nārhā vayaṁ hantuṁ dhārtarāṣhṭrān sa-bāndhavān
sva-janaṁ hi kathaṁ hatvā sukhinaḥ syāma mādhava		    
37 सञ्जय:      
38 सञ्जय: यद्यप्येते न पश्यन्ति लोभोपहतचेतस: |
कुलक्षयकृतं दोषं मित्रद्रोहे च पातकम् || 38||
कथं न ज्ञेयमस्माभि: पापादस्मान्निवर्तितुम् |
कुलक्षयकृतं दोषं प्रपश्यद्भिर्जनार्दन || 39||

yady apy ete na paśhyanti lobhopahata-chetasaḥ
kula-kṣhaya-kṛitaṁ doṣhaṁ mitra-drohe cha pātakam
kathaṁ na jñeyam asmābhiḥ pāpād asmān nivartitum
kula-kṣhaya-kṛitaṁ doṣhaṁ prapaśhyadbhir janārdana		    
39 सञ्जय:      
40 सञ्जय: कुलक्षये प्रणश्यन्ति कुलधर्मा: सनातना: |
धर्मे नष्टे कुलं कृत्स्नमधर्मोऽभिभवत्युत || 40||

kula-kṣhaye praṇaśhyanti kula-dharmāḥ sanātanāḥ
dharme naṣhṭe kulaṁ kṛitsnam adharmo bhibhavaty uta		    
41 सञ्जय: अधर्माभिभवात्कृष्ण प्रदुष्यन्ति कुलस्त्रिय: |
स्त्रीषु दुष्टासु वार्ष्णेय जायते वर्णसङ्कर: || 41||

adharmābhibhavāt kṛiṣhṇa praduṣhyanti kula-striyaḥ
strīṣhu duṣhṭāsu vārṣhṇeya jāyate varṇa-saṅkaraḥ		    
42 सञ्जय: सङ्करो नरकायैव कुलघ्नानां कुलस्य च |
पतन्ति पितरो ह्येषां लुप्तपिण्डोदकक्रिया: || 42||

saṅkaro narakāyaiva kula-ghnānāṁ kulasya cha
patanti pitaro hy eṣhāṁ lupta-piṇḍodaka-kriyāḥ		    
43 सञ्जय: दोषैरेतै: कुलघ्नानां वर्णसङ्करकारकै: |
उत्साद्यन्ते जातिधर्मा: कुलधर्माश्च शाश्वता: || 43||

doṣhair etaiḥ kula-ghnānāṁ varṇa-saṅkara-kārakaiḥ
utsādyante jāti-dharmāḥ kula-dharmāśh cha śhāśhvatāḥ		    
44 सञ्जय: उत्सन्नकुलधर्माणां मनुष्याणां जनार्दन |
नरकेऽनियतं वासो भवतीत्यनुशुश्रुम || 44||

utsanna-kula-dharmāṇāṁ manuṣhyāṇāṁ janārdana
narake niyataṁ vāso bhavatītyanuśhuśhruma		    
45 सञ्जय: अहो बत महत्पापं कर्तुं व्यवसिता वयम् |
यद्राज्यसुखलोभेन हन्तुं स्वजनमुद्यता: || 45||
यदि मामप्रतीकारमशस्त्रं शस्त्रपाणय: |
धार्तराष्ट्रा रणे हन्युस्तन्मे क्षेमतरं भवेत् || 46||

aho bata mahat pāpaṁ kartuṁ vyavasitā vayam
yad rājya-sukha-lobhena hantuṁ sva-janam udyatāḥ
yadi mām apratīkāram aśhastraṁ śhastra-pāṇayaḥ
dhārtarāṣhṭrā raṇe hanyus tan me kṣhemataraṁ bhavet		    
46 सञ्जय:      
47 सञ्जय: एवमुक्त्वार्जुन: सङ्ख्ये रथोपस्थ उपाविशत् |
विसृज्य सशरं चापं शोकसंविग्नमानस: || 47||

sañjaya uvācha
evam uktvārjunaḥ saṅkhye rathopastha upāviśhat
visṛijya sa-śharaṁ chāpaṁ śhoka-saṁvigna-mānasaḥ
